To say that the writer of such a book Js as mo-
dest as he is bold, is in other words to say that he
has earnestly investigated Nature. Bacon’s re-
mark of Aristotle, Audax simul et Pavidus, applies
to allsuch men. They are bold, in an assured cer-
tainty of the Laws which govern everything to
which knowledge can penetrate; they are humble,
in an awful contemplation of the Divine Author of
those Laws.
