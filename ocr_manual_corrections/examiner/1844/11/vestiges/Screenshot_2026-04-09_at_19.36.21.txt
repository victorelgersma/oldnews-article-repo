When we have arrived at that stage of the in-
guiry by which we discover that there is a general
adaptation of the mental constitution of man to the
circumstances in which he lives, as close as between
all the parts of nature to each other; when we find
that, for our physical constitution, the Almighty
Author of all things has destined it, like everything
else, to be developed from inherent qualities, and
to have a mode of action depending solely on its
own organization ;. the inquiry seems complete.
We have seen the masses of space formed by Law,
and in due time made theatres of existence for
plants and animals; we have seen in like manner
developed and sustained in action by Law, sensa-
tion, disposition, and intellect; and we have ob-
served that in the case of inorganic nature, the
Law is Gravitation, and in organic, Development.
But the question so inexpressibly interesting Te-
mains, of man’s final condition on the earth in his
relations to Supra-Mundane things.
