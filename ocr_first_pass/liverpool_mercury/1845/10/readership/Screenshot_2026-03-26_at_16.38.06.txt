PRESENT CIRCULATION.

Three months have now elapsed since we ventured
on the bold experiment of permanently enlarging
our Paper, by the issue of a Weekly Supplement,
and of increasing the price from Fivepence to Six-
pence, the additional penny being claimed in part
repayment of the additional outlay we were in-
curring for extra paper, extra stamps, and extra
printers’ wages; the balance of the cost, which is
upwards of £700 per annum, even tor asingle weekly
supplement, being undertaken to be borne by
ourselves, for the sake of giving larger type and
more general reading to our nynerous patrons.

During the progress of the experiment many in-
quiries have been made with regard to the result
some of our friends having had serious doubts, and
others ‘having felt no doubt, about it, whilst one
of our contemporaries has been pleased to represent
it ag an entire failure. We are happy, then, to
ye enabled to state, that such has been the public
approbation, of what has been considered a decided
improvement of our Paper, that its circulation
has not only not fallen off, notwithstanding the
advance of price, but has steadily increased, as will
appear from the following statement from our

publishing account :—
