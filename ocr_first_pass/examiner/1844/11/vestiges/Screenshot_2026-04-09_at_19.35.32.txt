The general result to which we are brought by
the close of the investigation is, that the simplest
and most primitive type of organic life, under a law
to which that of like-production is subordinate,
gave birth to the type next above it, that this again
produced the next higher, and so on to the very
highest. Applying this to the wonderful system
of circular analogies and affinities in nature (of
which Macleay is the principal author) it is found
that the only appearance of emperfection, as though
in this direction the laws of life were not yet ac-
complished, is in the circle to which man belongs.
Doubtless the ideas which rise in consequence are
not a little startling.
